Spring Herbs

Renewal, detoxification, and fresh beginnings

🌱 Spring Growing Tips

Start Seeds Indoors

Begin herbs like Tulsi and Neem indoors 6-8 weeks before the last frost for strong spring planting.

Soil Preparation

Enrich soil with compost and ensure good drainage for healthy root development as plants awaken.

Gradual Acclimation

Slowly introduce indoor-started herbs to outdoor conditions over 7-10 days to prevent shock.

Summer Herbs

Cooling, hydration, and sun protection

☀️ Summer Growing Tips

Morning Watering

Water deeply in early morning to reduce evaporation and prevent fungal diseases in humid conditions.

Provide Shade

Use shade cloth for delicate herbs during peak afternoon sun to prevent scorching.

Regular Harvesting

Frequent harvesting encourages bushier growth and prevents plants from going to seed too early.

Autumn Herbs

Immunity building and preparation for colder months

🍂 Autumn Growing Tips

Root Harvest Time

Autumn is ideal for harvesting root herbs like Ashwagandha when plant energy is concentrated below ground.

Protect from Frost

Cover tender herbs or bring pots indoors when frost warnings are issued to extend growing season.

Preserve Your Harvest

Dry or freeze abundant autumn harvests to ensure winter supply of your favorite medicinal herbs.

Winter Herbs

Warming, indoor gardening, and immune support

❄️ Winter Growing Tips

Indoor Herb Garden

Grow herbs like Ginger and Turmeric indoors near south-facing windows for adequate winter light.

Reduce Watering

Most herbs need less water in winter. Allow soil to dry slightly between waterings to prevent root rot.

Plan Next Season

Use winter months to plan your spring garden, order seeds, and prepare for the upcoming growing season.
